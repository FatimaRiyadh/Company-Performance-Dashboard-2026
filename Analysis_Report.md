# Analysis Report & Business Insights

## Introduction
This report provides a detailed analysis of the company's performance dataset for 2026. The goal is to transform raw sales and support data into actionable insights that help management make informed decisions regarding revenue optimization,
service quality improvement, and strategic workforce planning across Baghdad and Basra branches.

1) Which products drive the company's revenue growth, and which ones need marketing support?
   The phone drives the company's revenue, while accessories are what need marketing support.

2) What is the compliance rate with the Service Level Agreement (SLA), and is the company in the "safe zone"?
   The Within SLA compliance rate was approximately 70.83%, while the Breach SLA rate was 29.17%.
   The company is not entirely in a safe position; the fact that nearly 30% of tickets are outside the timeframe requires
   immediate management intervention to improve the efficiency of the support team or redistribute workload to reduce this percentage.

3) How does the performance of the Baghdad branch differ from that of the Basra branch in terms of profits and ticket processing speed?
   (Net Profit): Identical in both branches, with each branch achieving 4K.
   (SLA Status): The Baghdad branch outperformed, with its employees meeting deadlines for problem resolution at 75%, while the Basra branch's rate was only 66.67%.

4) What is the company's current profit margin, and is it within the target range?
   The company's current profit margin is 23.92%

5) When you click on the "Phones" category, how does (SLA) change?
   When you click on the Phones category, there is no change to the circular graphic of the SLA.
   This proves that product sales volume does not affect the efficiency of the technical support team,
   or that the technical problems customers encounter and maintenance tickets are not related to a specific type of product.

6) What is the net profit earned for each technical support ticket opened in the company?
   With a Total Net Profit of 9K and an overall volume of 24 support tickets across both branches, the company generates approximately
   $375 in net profit forevery customer ticket raised.
   This indicates a healthy balance, proving that the support operations and maintenance requests are not draining the company's financial returns.
